HEATMAP // AMSTERDAM
OPEN DATA | OPEN SOURCE | OPEN SCIENCE

ABOUT

An open initiative to map building heat readiness in Amsterdam. This project integrates physical building characteristics, energy efficiency data, and environmental factors to identify areas at risk from rising urban temperatures.

AMS INSTITUTE GEMEENTE AMSTERDAM MADE LIVING LAB

MISSION ONGOING

Identify physical characteristics that increase heat vulnerability in Amsterdam buildings. Collect and integrate open data at building level enabling data-driven decisions about urban heat mitigation.

Create custom heatmaps based on risk factors. Analyze relationships between building age, energy efficiency, and heat risk. Identify high-priority neighborhoods for intervention.

STATUS

Ongoing project actively mapping heat-related building characteristics across Amsterdam. Current coverage includes approximately 52,000 buildings with polygon data, predominantly in the city center. Data includes energy labels, building years, busy road locations, and neighborhood classifications.

Outskirts and some physical characteristics are still being collected. The goal is complete Amsterdam coverage with comprehensive heat-related metrics.
